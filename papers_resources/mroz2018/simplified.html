The Science of Perfecting Workplace Meetings

The Mroz et al. (2018) article functions as an academic review examining the pervasive problem of ineffective workplace meetings, noting that these frequently unsatisfactory gatherings cost organizations billions annually and negatively impact employee well-being.

The authors establish the rigorous, systematic field of meeting science, emphasizing that research in this area is necessary to transform meetings from organizational liabilities into valuable assets for strategy and problem-solving.

Three Critical Time Periods

This scientific approach frames meeting effectiveness around:

  • • Structural and design decisions made before the meeting
  • • Interactive behaviors of leaders and attendees during the meeting
  • • Necessary follow-up actions taken afterward

The article concludes that applying evidence-based best practices—such as using agendas, encouraging participation, and gathering feedback—can substantially improve meeting quality and overall employee job satisfaction.
